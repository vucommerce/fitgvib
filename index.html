About the Conference

The evolving landscape of India's fiscal architecture demands renewed scholarly attention, especially at a time when the nation stands at the threshold of a structural economic transformation. The introduction of Goods and Services Tax (GST) in 2017 marked a watershed moment in indirect taxation, bringing significant changes to tax administration, compliance, revenue generation, and inter-governmental fiscal relations. As India now moves towards GST 2.0 and aims towards Viksit Bharat by 2047, there is an urgent need to revisit the impact of revised tax structure, study sectoral implications, and assess the broader developmental outcomes of these reforms. Against this backdrop, the proposed seminar seeks to bring together academicians, policymakers, tax experts, industry practitioners, and research scholars to critically evaluate the future of India's taxation ecosystem and its interaction with capital flow patterns. This seminar will serve as a platform to deepen academic research, exchange empirical insights, and propose actionable policy frameworks relevant for the Government of India, state governments, industry stakeholders, and international institutions.

About the Department

Vidyasagar University is a reputed university in the Eastern region of India which has been catering to the needs of higher education since 1985. The Department of Commerce, among the oldest departments in the varsity presently offers M.Com. and Ph.D degrees. The department presently has a team of seven full-time Professors, each of whom has a Doctoral degree. The department publishes a journal, ‘Vidyasagar University Journal of Commerce’ (ISSN 0973-5917) which is indexed with Citefactor.org and EBSCO. The department also takes the credit of organizing Special Lectures regularly and conducting Research Methodology Workshops. This year the department is organising the 28th Annual Conference

Call for papers

The seminar invites theoretical, empirical, and policy-oriented papers on the folowing sub-themes:

Evaluation of Tax Structure
Structural Shifts in Consumption Patterns Post-GST
GST Reforms and Impact on MSMEs, Start-ups, and Entrepreneurship
GST Reforms and Impact on Rural and Urban Livelihoods and Employment
Law and GST: Legal and Judicial Perspectives
GST’s Regional Impact on Local Industries and Revenue Models
Sectoral Analysis After GST Reforms
GST Reforms, Digitalisation, Compliance, and Ease of Doing Business
GST Reforms and Social and Cultural Impact

This is only an indicative list. Any paper that does not fall under the above-mentioned sub-themes but is related to the theme of the conference is welcome.
